Instructions

The VHA Directive 1761(1) addresses limiting any risks posed to patients that come to the VA Hospitals.

Environment of Care (EOC) Committee

This committee includes leadership and management from many areas to include:

  • Logistics
  • EMS
  • Engineering
  • Safety
  • Infection Control
  • Nursing
  • Similarly critical areas

The role of this committee is to complete weekly rounds within the hospital to look for any deficiencies that can lead to patient safety issues.

An example of this would be finding expired product in the Clinical Storage areas. If expired items are found, they are written up and removed from the area. Logistics and clinical staff for that area are then instructed to look for expired items; reducing risk by properly rotating stock.

Facility Clinical Product Review Committee (CPRC)

The CPRC at each medical facility is tasked with:

  • Reviewing and approving all new expendable clinical items (prior to use for direct patient care)
  • Performing commodity standardization of all supplies (prior to use in direct patient care)
  • Ensuring staff training
  • Reporting all issues identified with item use related to product safety and/or efficacy

An example: A vendor wants to sell a newly-designed feeding tube to VA. The vendor presents the item to the committee with justification on why it is better than what is currently used.
